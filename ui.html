Doors of the Enchanted Forest
"On the way to Devil Mountain, you encounter an enchanted forest. The entrance is protected by the demon's magic."
There are 100 doors numbered 1 to 100 from left to right. All doors remain closed at the beginning. Toggling a door means: if it is open, close it; if it is closed, open it.

For each round $i$ where $1 \le i \le 100$, you toggle every $i^{\text{th}}$ door. More precisely:
Formal Definition
$$\text{Toggle door } d \text{ in round } i \iff i \mid d$$
A door is "safe" if and only if it is open after all 100 rounds. What is the sum of the indices of all safe doors?
Note: The answer is a positive integer. Express it as an exact value.

✏ DOODLE SLOT

Key insight - visible after solving
Door $d$ is toggled exactly once per divisor. It ends open iff it has an odd number of divisors - which happens iff $d$ is a perfect square. So sum the perfect squares $\le 100$:
$$\sum_{k=1}^{10} k^2 = \frac{10 \cdot 11 \cdot 21}{6} = 385$$

#004 · JADE CITY
Number Theory
Doors of the Enchanted Forest
"You encounter an enchanted forest. 100 doors stand before you, sealed by the demon's magic."
There are 100 doors numbered $1$ to $100$. All are closed initially. In round $i$, for $1 \le i \le 100$, you toggle every $i^{\text{th}}$ door. A door is safe if open at the end. Find the sum of indices of all safe doors.
$$\sum_{\substack{d=1 \\ \tau(d) \text{ odd}}}^{100} d = \sum_{k=1}^{10} k^2 = \frac{10 \cdot 11 \cdot 21}{6} = 385$$
Note: $\tau(d)$ denotes the number of positive divisors of $d$. Only perfect squares have $\tau(d)$ odd.
#005 · JADE CITY
Geometry
The Double Death Ritual
"You and your friends must stand at precise positions to cast the "Double Death" spell and defeat the demon."
Let $M$ be the center of circumcircle $(P_2P_3P_4)$ with radius $10$. Given $P_2P_3 = P_4P_3$ and $\angle P_2P_3P_4 = 90°$. Let $P_1$ be the center of a circle tangent to line $P_2P_4$ at $A$ and internally tangent to $(P_2P_3P_4)$ at $B$. Given $P_3A = 13$, find $AB = \tfrac{a}{b}$ in lowest terms, and compute $a + b$.
$$\triangle BP_3P_4 \sim \triangle P_4P_3A \implies \frac{BP_3}{P_3P_4} = \frac{P_4P_3}{P_3A}$$ $$AB = \frac{P_3P_4^2}{P_3A} - P_3A = \frac{(20/\sqrt{2})^2}{13} - 13 = \frac{200}{13} - 13 = \frac{31}{13}$$ $$a + b = 31 + 13 = \boxed{44}$$
The answer 44 = "double death" (四四) in Chinese - the title's hidden meaning.
#003 · JADE CITY
Combinatorics / DP
The Carriage Fee Distribution
"Four demon hunters, each with one $1, $2, and $3 coin - how many ways to split the $8 carriage fee?"
Four players each choose a coin from $\{1, 2, 3\}$. How many ordered tuples $(p_1, p_2, p_3, p_4)$ satisfy $p_1 + p_2 + p_3 + p_4 = 8$?
$$\text{Let } dp_{i,j} = \#\text{ways first }i\text{ players sum to }j$$ $$dp_{i,j} = dp_{i-1,j-1} + dp_{i-1,j-2} + dp_{i-1,j-3}$$ $$dp_{4,8} = dp_{3,7} + dp_{3,6} + dp_{3,5} = 6 + 7 + 6 = \boxed{19}$$
3 + 8 + 5 = 16 = 4². The number 4 (death) appears again in the answer structure.
BONUS · PRACTICE
Algebra
The Scribe's Polynomial
"A scribe encoded a message in the roots of a polynomial. Decode the number of real roots to read the message."
Let $f(x) = x^2 + bx + c$ where $b, c \in \mathbb{Z}$. Find the number of integer pairs $(b, c)$ such that $f(f(x)) = 0$ has exactly 3 distinct real roots.
$$f(f(x)) = 0 \implies f(x) = r_1 \text{ or } f(x) = r_2$$ \text{where } r_1, r_2 \text{ are roots of } f. $$\text{For exactly 3 real roots: one } f(x)=r_i \text{ has a double root}$$ $$\Rightarrow \Delta = b^2 - 4c = 0 \text{ and } r_1 = r_2 \text{ (one real root of } f)$$
This is an AMC/AIME level problem. The complete solution uses Vieta's formulas + discriminant analysis.
